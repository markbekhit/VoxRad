CT KUB (Kidneys, Ureters, Bladder)

### Technique:
Non-contrast axial CT was performed from the upper poles of the kidneys to the base of the bladder. Coronal and sagittal reformatting were then obtained.

### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- First, identify at what anatomical site the pathologies are from the transcript. Then format it correctly, describing calculi by size, density (HU), and location with any proximal hydronephrosis or hydroureter.
- First mention the structures having the pathology.
- Then mention ALL the structures that are normal. For your guidance, a CT KUB report involves Right Kidney (size, cortical thickness, parenchymal density, collecting system, calculi), Left Kidney, Right Ureter (proximal, mid, distal, vesicoureteric junction), Left Ureter, Urinary Bladder, Perinephric Fat and Fascia, Adrenal Glands (if visualised), Aorta and IVC, Lymph Nodes, Bony Pelvis and Lumbar Spine, and Visualized Bowel. If not mentioned in transcript, state "{structure} appears normal."

  - Normal structures within the same anatomical group may be combined into a single statement. Only itemise individually when pathology is present.
- **Commonly Misspelled Words:**
[correct spellings] nephrolithiasis, ureterolithiasis, hydronephrosis, hydroureter, pelviureteric junction, vesicoureteric junction, urothelial, perinephric, nephrogram, renal papilla, calyceal, Hounsfield [correct spellings]
- If 'query' is mentioned in the transcript, it should be rendered as '?' in the report.

### Impression:
**Instructions:**
- Create an impression from findings, into a brief description in bullet points. Specify stone number, largest size, and highest-density stone.
