### Exam:
MRI Lumbar Spine

### Technique:
MRI of the lumbar spine was performed using T1-weighted, T2-weighted, and STIR sequences in sagittal and axial planes. [If contrast: Post-gadolinium T1-weighted sequences were also obtained.]

### Clinical Details
**Instructions:**
- Record clinical details exactly as dictated. Do not rephrase or reformulate the wording.
- **Exclude** patient name, date of birth, age, gender, and MRN/accession numbers from the report, even if mentioned in the transcript.
- **If not provided:** 'No clinical details provided.'

### Comparison:
**Instructions:**
- **If provided:** List the prior imaging studies mentioned (modality and date/year if stated). Do not summarise changes here — comparison findings belong in the Findings section.
- **If not provided:** 'No prior imaging available for comparison.'

### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- First, identify at what anatomical site the pathologies are from the transcript. Then format it correctly, describing disc morphology (bulge/protrusion/extrusion/sequestration), signal change, degree of canal/foraminal/lateral recess compromise, and nerve root involvement.
- First mention the structures having the pathology.
- Then mention ALL the structures that are normal. For your guidance, an MRI Lumbar Spine report involves Vertebral Bodies (L1–L5, S1), Bone Marrow Signal, Intervertebral Discs (L1/2 through L5/S1 — disc height, T2 signal, disc morphology), End Plates, Facet Joints (bilateral, each level), Spinal Canal (AP diameter), Thecal Sac, Cauda Equina Nerve Roots, Lateral Recesses (bilateral), Neural Foramina (bilateral, each level), Ligamentum Flavum, Sacroiliac Joints, Paraspinal Muscles, and Visualized Abdominal Structures. If not mentioned, state "{structure} appears normal."

  - Describe each abnormal disc level specifically. Normal disc levels may be grouped: e.g. "Remaining disc levels show preserved height and normal T2 signal." Other structures (thecal sac, cord/cauda equina, facet joints, paraspinal muscles) may be combined if normal.
    For instance:
    - **L4/L5:** Left paracentral disc protrusion abutting the traversing left L5 nerve root with mild left lateral recess narrowing.
    - **L5/S1:** Broad-based disc bulge with mild bilateral foraminal narrowing.
    - **Remaining Levels (L1/2–L3/4):** Normal disc height and T2 signal, no significant canal or foraminal compromise.
    - **Thecal Sac and Cauda Equina:** Adequate calibre, no nerve root compression at remaining levels.
    - and so on...
- **Commonly Misspelled Words:**
[correct spellings] spondylolysis, spondylolisthesis, foraminal stenosis, lateral recess, cauda equina, ligamentum flavum, Modic, annular fissure, protrusion, extrusion, sequestration, facet hypertrophy [correct spellings]
- If 'query' is mentioned in the transcript, it should be rendered as '?' in the report.

### Impression:
**Instructions:**
- Create an impression from findings, into a brief description in bullet points.
- Include only clinically relevant findings. Minor incidental findings that are fully described in the body do not need to be restated here.
- For a normal/negative study, include a closing statement where appropriate, e.g. 'No cause for the patient's symptoms identified on this examination.'
- **Sample:**
  - Lumbar spondylosis most significant at [level] with [disc protrusion / canal stenosis] affecting [nerve root].
  - No acute fracture or marrow signal abnormality.
