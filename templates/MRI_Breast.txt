### Exam:
MRI Breast

### Technique:
MRI of both breasts was performed in the prone position using bilateral dedicated breast coils. Sequences included T1-weighted, T2-weighted fat-suppressed, and dynamic contrast-enhanced T1-weighted sequences (pre-contrast and multiple post-contrast phases) following intravenous gadolinium administration. Subtraction and MIP reconstructions were reviewed.

### Clinical Details
**Instructions:**
- Record clinical details exactly as dictated. Do not rephrase or reformulate the wording.
- **Exclude** patient name, date of birth, age, gender, and MRN/accession numbers from the report, even if mentioned in the transcript.
- **If not provided:** 'No clinical details provided.'

### Comparison:
**Instructions:**
- **If provided:** List the prior imaging studies mentioned (modality and date/year if stated). Do not summarise changes here — comparison findings belong in the Findings section.
- **If not provided:** 'No prior imaging available for comparison.'

### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- First, identify at what anatomical site the pathologies are from the transcript. Then format it correctly using ACR BI-RADS lexicon — describe mass morphology (shape, margin, internal enhancement), non-mass enhancement (distribution, internal pattern), and kinetic curve characteristics (initial and delayed phases).
- First mention the structures having the pathology.
- Then mention ALL the structures that are normal. For your guidance, an MRI Breast report involves Right Breast (each quadrant/clock position, nipple, subareolar region), Left Breast, Background Parenchymal Enhancement (BPE — minimal/mild/moderate/marked), Axillary Lymph Nodes (bilateral), Skin and Nipple–Areolar Complex, Pectoral Muscles, Chest Wall, Sternum, and Visualized Upper Abdomen. If not mentioned, state "{structure} appears normal."

  - Normal structures within the same anatomical group may be combined into a single statement. Only itemise individually when pathology is present.
- **Commonly Misspelled Words:**
[correct spellings] BI-RADS, kinetics, enhancement, background parenchymal enhancement, non-mass enhancement, spiculated, circumscribed, heterogeneous, washout, plateau, persistent, subareolar, axillary [correct spellings]
- If 'query' is mentioned in the transcript, it should be rendered as '?' in the report.
- Assign a final BI-RADS category (0–6) in the impression.

### Impression:
**Instructions:**
- Create an impression from findings, into a brief description in bullet points. Include BI-RADS category.
- Include only clinically relevant findings. Minor incidental findings that are fully described in the body do not need to be restated here.
- For a normal/negative study, include a closing statement where appropriate, e.g. 'No cause for the patient's symptoms identified on this examination.'
- **Sample:**
  - Right breast 10 o'clock mass with suspicious morphology and kinetics — MRI BI-RADS 5.
  - Recommend biopsy for histopathological confirmation.
