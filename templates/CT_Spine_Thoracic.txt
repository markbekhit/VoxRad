### Exam:
CT Thoracic Spine

### Technique:
Axial cuts were obtained from the cervicothoracic junction to the thoracolumbar junction. Sagittal and coronal reformatting were then obtained. Bone and soft tissue window reconstructions were reviewed.

### Clinical Details
**Instructions:**
- Record clinical details exactly as dictated. Do not rephrase or reformulate the wording.
- **Exclude** patient name, date of birth, age, gender, and MRN/accession numbers from the report, even if mentioned in the transcript.
- **If not provided:** 'No clinical details provided.'

### Comparison:
**Instructions:**
- **If provided:** List the prior imaging studies mentioned (modality and date/year if stated). Do not summarise changes here — comparison findings belong in the Findings section.
- **If not provided:** 'No prior imaging available for comparison.'

### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- First, identify at what anatomical site the pathologies are from the transcript. Then format it correctly, describing its morphology and any neural compromise.
- First mention the structures having the pathology.
- Then mention ALL the structures that are normal. For your guidance, a CT Thoracic Spine report involves Vertebral Bodies (T1–T12), Intervertebral Disc Spaces, Facet Joints, Pedicles and Posterior Elements, Rib Articulations, Spinal Canal, Neural Foramina, Paraspinal Soft Tissues, and Visualized Thoracic Organs. If not mentioned in transcript, state "{structure} appears normal."

  - Normal structures within the same anatomical group may be combined into a single statement. Only itemise individually when pathology is present.
- **Commonly Misspelled Words:**
[correct spellings] vertebra, spondylosis, spondylolisthesis, kyphosis, scoliosis, pedicle, paraspinal, costovertebral, osteophyte, compression fracture, Schmorl's node [correct spellings]
- If 'query' is mentioned in the transcript, it should be rendered as '?' in the report.

### Impression:
**Instructions:**
- Create an impression from findings, into a brief description in bullet points.
- Include only clinically relevant findings. Minor incidental findings that are fully described in the body do not need to be restated here.
- For a normal/negative study, include a closing statement where appropriate, e.g. 'No cause for the patient's symptoms identified on this examination.'
- **Sample:**
  - Thoracic spondylosis with [level-specific] findings.
  - *No acute fracture or significant cord compromise identified.*
