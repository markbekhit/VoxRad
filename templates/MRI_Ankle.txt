### Exam:
MRI Ankle

### Technique:
MRI of the [right/left] ankle was performed using PD-weighted fat-suppressed, T2-weighted, T1-weighted, and STIR sequences in coronal, sagittal, and axial planes.

### Clinical Details
**Instructions:**
- Record clinical details exactly as dictated. Do not rephrase or reformulate the wording.
- **Exclude** patient name, date of birth, age, gender, and MRN/accession numbers from the report, even if mentioned in the transcript.
- **If not provided:** 'No clinical details provided.'

### Comparison:
**Instructions:**
- **If provided:** List the prior imaging studies mentioned (modality and date/year if stated). Do not summarise changes here — comparison findings belong in the Findings section.
- **If not provided:** 'No prior imaging available for comparison.'

### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- Describe pathological structures specifically. Normal structures within the same anatomical group may be combined into a single statement — do not force a separate bullet for every structure when all are normal.
- Use precise radiology terminology for normal structures:
  - Tendons → "intact, normal calibre and signal"
  - Ligaments → "intact"
  - Articular cartilage → "intact, no focal chondral defect"
  - Joint effusion → "no significant tibiotalar or subtalar effusion"
  - Bony structures → "no fracture or aggressive bony lesion"
  - Bone marrow → "no bone marrow oedema"

Cover all groups in this order:

**Tendons**
- Medial: Posterior tibial, Flexor digitorum longus, Flexor hallucis longus (tarsal tunnel)
- Lateral: Peroneus longus and brevis
- Anterior: Tibialis anterior, Extensor hallucis longus, Extensor digitorum longus
- Posterior: Achilles tendon (describe thickness, continuity, tendinosis, partial vs full thickness tear, insertional enthesopathy, retrocalcaneal bursitis)

**Plantar Fascia**
- Default: "Plantar fascia unremarkable, no thickening or signal abnormality."

**Ligaments**
- Lateral: ATFL, CFL, PTFL
- Medial (deltoid): superficial and deep components
- Syndesmosis: AITFL, PITFL, interosseous ligament
- Spring ligament, bifurcate ligament, sinus tarsi contents

**Articular Cartilage and Osteochondral Surfaces**
- Comment specifically on the talar dome (medial and lateral shoulder) for osteochondral lesions. If pathology is present, grade and localise (medial/lateral, anterior/central/posterior).

**Bony Structures**
- ALWAYS use the radiologist's exact dictated words for bony/marrow findings, whether normal or abnormal.
- Only use the default "Normal bony alignment. Bone marrow signal is normal. No fracture or aggressive osseous lesion." if the radiologist said NOTHING about bony structures.
- Address specifically: tibia, fibula, talus, calcaneus, tarsal navicular, cuboid, cuneiforms, metatarsal bases, accessory bones (os trigonum, os peroneum).

**Joint Effusion and Bursae**
- Tibiotalar, subtalar (posterior/anterior), talonavicular, calcaneocuboid joints
- Retrocalcaneal bursa, retro-Achilles bursa

**Soft Tissues**
- Default: "Surrounding soft tissues unremarkable."

### Impression:
**Instructions:**
- Concise bullet-point summary of key findings.
- Include only clinically relevant findings. Minor incidental findings that are fully described in the body do not need to be restated here.
- For a normal/negative study, include a closing statement where appropriate, e.g. 'No cause for the patient's symptoms identified on this examination.'
- **Sample:**
  - High-grade partial-thickness tear of the ATFL. CFL intact.
  - Mild Achilles tendinosis. No tear.
  - No osteochondral lesion. No significant joint effusion.
