### THIS IS THE SAMPLE TEMPLATE TO BE USED FOR THE TRANSCRIPT ###

### Exam:
MRI Knee

### Technique:
MRI of the [right/left] knee was performed using PD-weighted fat-suppressed, T2-weighted, T1-weighted, and STIR sequences in coronal, sagittal, and axial planes.

### Clinical Details
**Instructions:**
- **If provided:**
  - 'The patient presented with [describe symptoms or reason for the examination], with a medical history notable for [mention relevant medical conditions].'
  - **Exclude** name, age, gender, and IDs anywhere in the report, even if provided in the transcript.

- **If not provided:**
  - 'No clinical details provided.'

### Comparison:
**Instructions:**
- **If provided:** Mention any prior imaging studies and briefly note significant changes or findings compared to previous examinations.
- **If not provided:** 'No prior imaging scans/reports available for comparison.'

### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- First, identify at what anatomical site the pathologies are from the transcript. Then format it correctly, describing morphology, signal characteristics, and grade of injury.
- First mention the structures having the pathology.
- Then mention ALL the structures that are normal. For your guidance, an MRI Knee report involves Medial Meniscus (anterior horn, body, posterior horn), Lateral Meniscus (anterior horn, body, posterior horn), Anterior Cruciate Ligament (ACL), Posterior Cruciate Ligament (PCL), Medial Collateral Ligament (MCL), Lateral Collateral Ligament (LCL), Posterolateral Corner Structures, Articular Cartilage (medial femoral condyle, lateral femoral condyle, medial tibial plateau, lateral tibial plateau, patella, trochlear groove), Patellofemoral Joint, Quadriceps Tendon, Patellar Tendon, Joint Effusion, Hoffa's Fat Pad, Popliteal Fossa, Baker's Cyst, Bony Structures (femur, tibia, fibula, patella), and Bone Marrow Oedema. If not mentioned, state "{structure} appears normal."

  - DO NOT JUST SAY "No other structures mentioned, assumed to be normal."
  - DO NOT club rest of the structures to be normal.
  - BUT RATHER mention each in a new bullet point.
    For instance:
    - **Medial Meniscus:** Grade III signal extending to the inferior articular surface of the posterior horn, in keeping with a horizontal tear.
    - **ACL:** Intact with normal signal and tension.
    - **Articular Cartilage:** Full-thickness chondral defect at the medial femoral condyle.
    - and so on...
- **Commonly Misspelled Words:**
[correct spellings] meniscus, meniscal, cruciate, collateral, chondral, osteochondral, patellofemoral, trochlear, Hoffa, posterolateral, osteophyte, subchondral oedema [correct spellings]
- If 'query' is mentioned in the transcript, it should be rendered as '?' in the report.

### Impression:
**Instructions:**
- Create an impression from findings, into a brief description in bullet points.
- **Sample:**
  - Posterior horn medial meniscal tear.
  - ACL intact. No significant ligamentous injury.

### Advice/Recommendation(s):
**Instructions:**
- Offer recommendations for further evaluation or management based on the impression of the report.
- 'Orthopaedic consultation is advised for surgical planning of meniscal tear/ligament injury.'
- 'Physiotherapy may be appropriate for stable ligamentous injuries.'
- Otherwise offer a general statement 'Clinical correlation is recommended for further evaluation and management.'
