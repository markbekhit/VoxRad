### THIS IS CONTINUATION OF SYSTEM PROMPT ###
### THIS IS THE SAMPLE TEMPLATE TO BE USED FOR THE TRANSCRIPT ###

This template produces a free-prose radiology report. Unlike the structured templates, Findings is narrative prose with no bold sub-headings or per-structure bullets.

---

### Exam:
- If an exam type is stated in the dictation, use it verbatim (e.g. "Chest radiograph", "CT brain"). Otherwise, omit this heading entirely — do not invent one.

### Clinical Details
**Instructions:**
- Record clinical details exactly as dictated. Do not rephrase or reformulate the wording.
- **Exclude** patient name, date of birth, age, gender, and MRN/accession numbers from the report, even if mentioned in the transcript.
- **If not provided:** 'No clinical details provided.'

### Comparison:
**Instructions:**
- **If provided:** List the prior imaging studies mentioned (modality and date/year if stated). Do not summarise changes here — comparison findings belong in the Findings section.
- **If not provided:** 'No prior imaging available for comparison.'

### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- Present findings as a single flowing prose paragraph (or two short paragraphs if the dictation is long). **Do NOT** create bold sub-headings, per-structure bullets, or any structured sub-sections within Findings.
- Use the radiologist's dictated wording where possible. Correct only grammar, punctuation, sentence flow, and radiology spelling — do not paraphrase or re-order clinical content.
- **Only report structures the radiologist mentioned.** Do not volunteer default-normal statements about structures that were not dictated. (This is the key difference from structured templates.)
- If the radiologist dictates a brief normal summary (e.g. "normal chest X-ray" or "unremarkable CT brain"), expand that to a single canonical normal sentence in prose form — do not produce a structured block of negatives.
- Begin with the most clinically relevant finding(s), followed by supporting findings.

### Impression:
**Instructions:**
- Concise bullet-point summary.
- Include only clinically relevant findings. Minor incidental findings that are fully described in the body do not need to be restated here.
- For a normal/negative study, include a closing statement where appropriate, e.g. 'No cause for the patient's symptoms identified on this examination.'
