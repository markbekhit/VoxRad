### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- First, identify at what anatomical site the pathologies are from the transcript. Then format it correctly, describing lesion size, segment location, signal characteristics (T1, T2, DWI), and enhancement pattern (arterial enhancement, washout, capsule).
- First mention the structures having the pathology.
- Then mention ALL the structures that are normal. For your guidance, an MRI Abdomen–Liver report involves Liver (lobes, segments I–VIII, parenchymal signal, focal lesions), Gallbladder and Biliary System (intrahepatic and extrahepatic bile ducts), Spleen, Pancreas (head, neck, body, tail, main pancreatic duct), Kidneys and Adrenal Glands, Portal Vein and Hepatic Veins, Inferior Vena Cava, Bowel (if visualised), Mesentery and Peritoneum, Ascites, Lymph Nodes, and Visualized Bony Structures. If not mentioned, state "{structure} appears normal."

  - Normal structures within the same anatomical group may be combined. Only itemise individually when pathology is present.
    For instance:
    - **Liver:** Segment VI lesion measuring [X] cm — T1 hypointense, T2 hyperintense, arterial phase enhancement with portal venous washout and capsule appearance, consistent with hepatocellular carcinoma (LI-RADS 5).
    - **Portal Vein:** Patent, no thrombosis.
    - **Spleen:** Normal in size and signal.
    - and so on...
- **Commonly Misspelled Words:**
[correct spellings] hepatocellular carcinoma, cholangiocarcinoma, haemangioma, focal nodular hyperplasia, hepatic adenoma, washout, arterial enhancement, hepatobiliary phase, cirrhosis, portal hypertension, cholestasis, LI-RADS [correct spellings]
- If 'query' is mentioned in the transcript, it should be rendered as '?' in the report.
- Apply LI-RADS categorisation for hepatic lesions in patients with cirrhosis or chronic liver disease if applicable.
