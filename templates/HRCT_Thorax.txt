HRCT Thorax

High-resolution CT of the thorax was performed using thin-section axial acquisition from the thoracic inlet to the diaphragm. Coronal and sagittal reformatting were then obtained. Prone images were obtained where applicable.

### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- Present findings using the bold section headers below in this exact order. Do NOT create new headings or rename sections.
- For each section: if the radiologist mentioned it, reproduce their exact words. If not mentioned, use an appropriate normal descriptor.
- Describe interstitial pattern using standard HRCT terminology: ground-glass opacity (GGO), reticulation, honeycombing, traction bronchiectasis, consolidation, nodules (centrilobular/perilymphatic/random), tree-in-bud, air trapping.
- State distribution: upper/mid/lower zone predominance; central/peripheral; unilateral/bilateral.

**Lung Parenchyma**
- Default: "Lung parenchyma clear bilaterally. No ground-glass opacity, consolidation, reticulation, or nodules."
- If pathology: describe pattern, distribution, and zone predominance precisely.

**Airways**
- Default: "No bronchiectasis or bronchial wall thickening. No endobronchial lesion."

**Pleura**
- Default: "No pleural effusion, pneumothorax, or pleural thickening."

**Mediastinum and Hila**
- Default: "Mediastinum and hila unremarkable. No lymphadenopathy."

**Heart and Pericardium**
- Default: "Heart normal in size. No pericardial effusion."

**Chest Wall**
- Default: "Chest wall unremarkable."

**Bony Thorax**
- ALWAYS use the radiologist's exact dictated words for bony findings. Only use the default "Bony thorax intact. No lytic or sclerotic lesion." if the radiologist said NOTHING about bones.

**Visualised Upper Abdomen**
