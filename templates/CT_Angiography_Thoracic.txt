### Exam:
CT Pulmonary Angiography / CT Thoracic Aorta

### Technique:
Axial cuts were obtained from the thoracic inlet to the upper abdomen following bolus intravenous contrast administration. Scans were timed to the pulmonary arterial / aortic phase using bolus tracking. Coronal and sagittal reformatting, including maximum intensity projection (MIP) reconstructions, were obtained.

### Clinical Details
**Instructions:**
- **If provided:**
  - 'The patient presented with [describe symptoms or reason for the examination], with a medical history notable for [mention relevant medical conditions].'
  - **Exclude** name, age, gender, and IDs anywhere in the report, even if provided in the transcript.

- **If not provided:**
  - 'No clinical details provided.'

### Comparison:
**Instructions:**
- **If provided:** Mention any prior imaging studies and briefly note significant changes or findings compared to previous examinations.
- **If not provided:** 'No prior imaging scans/reports available for comparison.'

### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- First, identify at what anatomical site the pathologies are from the transcript. Then format it correctly.
- First mention the structures having the pathology.
- Then mention ALL the structures that are normal. For your guidance, a CT Pulmonary Angiography / CT Thoracic Aorta report involves Main Pulmonary Arteries (main, left, right), Lobar and Segmental Pulmonary Arteries, Thoracic Aorta (root, ascending, arch, descending), Great Vessels (innominate, carotid, subclavian), Pulmonary Veins, Cardiac Chambers, Pericardium, Lungs and Pleura, Mediastinum and Lymph Nodes, Chest Wall, and Visualized Upper Abdomen. If not mentioned in transcript, state "{structure} appears normal."

  - DO NOT JUST SAY "No other structures mentioned, assumed to be normal."
  - DO NOT club rest of the structures to be normal.
  - BUT RATHER mention each in a new bullet point.
    For instance:
    - **Pulmonary Arteries:** No filling defect to suggest pulmonary embolism.
    - **Thoracic Aorta:** Normal calibre throughout, no dissection flap or aneurysmal dilatation.
    - **Lungs:** No parenchymal consolidation or pleural effusion.
    - and so on...
- **Commonly Misspelled Words:**
[correct spellings] pulmonary embolism, dissection, aneurysm, thrombosis, saddle embolus, intraluminal, occlusion, stenosis, haemothorax, pericardial effusion, aortic arch, innominate [correct spellings]
- If 'query' is mentioned in the transcript, it should be rendered as '?' in the report.

### Impression:
**Instructions:**
- Create an impression from findings, into a brief description in bullet points.
- **Sample:**
  - No evidence of pulmonary embolism.
  - Normal thoracic aorta; no dissection or aneurysm.

### Advice/Recommendation(s):
**Instructions:**
- Offer recommendations for further evaluation or management based on the impression of the report.
- 'Anticoagulation therapy should be considered in confirmed pulmonary embolism — discuss with the clinical team.'
- 'Vascular surgery consultation is advised for significant aortic pathology.'
- If no significant findings are identified, state 'No further radiological workup required.'
