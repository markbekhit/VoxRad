### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- First, identify at what anatomical site the pathologies are from the transcript. Then format it correctly, describing its morphology and any neural compromise.
- First mention the structures having the pathology.
- Then mention ALL the structures that are normal. For your guidance, a CT Cervical Spine report involves Vertebral Bodies (C1–C7), Intervertebral Disc Spaces, Facet Joints, Pedicles and Posterior Elements, Spinal Canal and Thecal Sac, Neural Foramina (bilateral, each level), Uncovertebral Joints, Prevertebral Soft Tissues, Atlantoaxial Joint and Odontoid Process, and Visualized Posterior Fossa and Cervicothoracic Junction. If the transcript doesn't mention anything about a certain structure or level, mention that it "appears normal."
