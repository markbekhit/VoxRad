### Exam:
Ultrasound Pelvis

### Technique:
[Transabdominal / Transvaginal / Transrectal / Combined transabdominal and transvaginal] ultrasound of the pelvis was performed.

### Clinical Details
**Instructions:**
- Record clinical details exactly as dictated. Do not rephrase or reformulate the wording.
- **Exclude** patient name, date of birth, age, gender, and MRN/accession numbers from the report, even if mentioned in the transcript.
- **If not provided:** 'No clinical details provided.'

### Comparison:
**Instructions:**
- **If provided:** List the prior imaging studies mentioned (modality and date/year if stated). Do not summarise changes here — comparison findings belong in the Findings section.
- **If not provided:** 'No prior imaging available for comparison.'

### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- First, identify at what anatomical site the pathologies are from the transcript. Then format it correctly.
- First mention the structures having the pathology.
- Then mention ALL the structures that are normal. For your guidance, a Pelvic Ultrasound report (female) involves Uterus (position — anteverted/retroverted, size in three dimensions, myometrial echotexture, fibroids), Endometrium (thickness, echogenicity, regularity), Cervix, Right Ovary (size in three dimensions, follicles, dominant follicle, cysts, masses), Left Ovary, Adnexae (free fluid, masses), Pouch of Douglas (free fluid), Urinary Bladder (wall, volume), and Visualized Bowel. [For male patients: Urinary Bladder, Prostate (size, echotexture, residual volume), Seminal Vesicles.] If not mentioned, state "{structure} appears normal."

  - Normal structures within the same anatomical group may be combined into a single statement. Only itemise individually when pathology is present.
- **Commonly Misspelled Words:**
[correct spellings] endometrium, myometrium, fibroid, leiomyoma, anteverted, retroverted, follicle, corpus luteum, hydrosalpinx, endometriosis, adenomyosis, ovarian torsion, polycystic [correct spellings]
- If 'query' is mentioned in the transcript, it should be rendered as '?' in the report.

### Impression:
**Instructions:**
- Create an impression from findings, into a brief description in bullet points.
- Include only clinically relevant findings. Minor incidental findings that are fully described in the body do not need to be restated here.
- For a normal/negative study, include a closing statement where appropriate, e.g. 'No cause for the patient's symptoms identified on this examination.'
- **Sample:**
  - Uterine fibroid as described. Normal endometrium.
  - Normal bilateral ovaries. No adnexal mass or free fluid.
