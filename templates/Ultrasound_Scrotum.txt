### Exam:
Ultrasound Scrotum

### Technique:
Greyscale and colour Doppler ultrasound of the scrotum was performed with high-frequency linear transducer. Comparison images of both testes in the same field were obtained to assess symmetry of vascularity.

### Clinical Details

### Comparison:
**Instructions:**

### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- Present findings using the bold section headers below in this exact order. Do NOT create new headings or rename sections.
- For each structure: if the radiologist mentioned it, reproduce their exact words. If not mentioned, use the default.
- Measure each testis in three dimensions and report volumes. Describe focal lesions by size, location, echogenicity, vascularity, and cystic vs solid character.

**Right Testis**
- Default: "Right testis measures [__] x [__] x [__] mm (volume [__] mL). Normal size and homogeneous echotexture. Normal vascularity on colour Doppler. No focal testicular lesion. Tunica albuginea intact."

**Left Testis**
- Default: "Left testis measures [__] x [__] x [__] mm (volume [__] mL). Normal size and homogeneous echotexture. Normal vascularity on colour Doppler. No focal testicular lesion. Tunica albuginea intact."

**Epididymides**
- Default: "Bilateral epididymides (head, body, tail) normal in size and echogenicity. No mass or hyperaemia. No epididymal cyst."
- If epididymo-orchitis: describe hyperaemia, wall thickening, and associated reactive hydrocele.

**Spermatic Cords**
- Default: "Spermatic cords unremarkable. No varicocele; veins measure < 2 mm with no reflux on Valsalva."
- If varicocele: maximum vein diameter, grade (I–III), and reflux pattern with Valsalva.

**Scrotal Wall and Sac**
- Default: "Scrotal wall normal thickness. No hydrocele, pyocele, or haematocele."
- If hydrocele: describe loculations and internal echoes.

**Colour/Spectral Doppler**
- Default: "Symmetric, normal intratesticular arterial flow with preserved diastolic component bilaterally. No testicular torsion."
- If torsion suspected: explicitly comment on presence/absence of intratesticular flow, whirlpool sign of the cord, and heterogeneity of parenchyma.

**Inguinal Regions**
- Default: "No inguinal hernia on cough/Valsalva. No enlarged inguinal lymph nodes."

### Impression:
**Instructions:**

- Include only clinically relevant findings. Minor incidental findings that are fully described in the body do not need to be restated here.
