### Exam:
MRI Wrist

### Technique:
MRI of the [right/left] wrist was performed using PD-weighted fat-suppressed, T2-weighted, T1-weighted, and STIR sequences in coronal, sagittal, and axial planes. [If MR arthrogram: Intra-articular gadolinium was administered; fat-suppressed T1-weighted sequences were obtained in three planes.]

### Clinical Details
**Instructions:**
- Record clinical details exactly as dictated. Do not rephrase or reformulate the wording.
- **Exclude** patient name, date of birth, age, gender, and MRN/accession numbers from the report, even if mentioned in the transcript.
- **If not provided:** 'No clinical details provided.'

### Comparison:
**Instructions:**
- **If provided:** List the prior imaging studies mentioned (modality and date/year if stated). Do not summarise changes here — comparison findings belong in the Findings section.
- **If not provided:** 'No prior imaging available for comparison.'

### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- Describe pathological structures specifically. Normal structures within the same anatomical group may be combined into a single statement — do not force a separate bullet for every structure when all are normal.

Cover all groups in this order:

**Carpal Bones and Alignment**
- Scaphoid, lunate, triquetrum, pisiform, trapezium, trapezoid, capitate, hamate (with hook)
- Comment on alignment on sagittal sequences: scapholunate angle, capitolunate angle — note DISI, VISI, SLAC, SNAC patterns if present.
- ALWAYS use the radiologist's exact dictated words for bony/marrow findings, whether normal or abnormal.
- Default: "Normal carpal alignment. Normal marrow signal. No fracture, AVN, or aggressive bony lesion."

**Triangular Fibrocartilage Complex (TFCC)**
- Default: "TFCC intact. No tear."
- If pathology: classify per Palmer (traumatic 1A–1D, degenerative 2A–2E) and localise.

**Intrinsic Ligaments**
- Scapholunate ligament (dorsal, proximal, volar bands)
- Lunotriquetral ligament
- Default: "Scapholunate and lunotriquetral ligaments intact."

**Extrinsic Ligaments**
- Radioscaphocapitate, long radiolunate, short radiolunate, ulnolunate, ulnotriquetral, dorsal intercarpal
- Default: "Extrinsic carpal ligaments intact."

**Tendons**
- Extensor compartments 1–6 (1: APL/EPB; 2: ECRL/ECRB; 3: EPL; 4: EDC/EIP; 5: EDM; 6: ECU)
- Flexor tendons (FCR, FCU, flexor digitorum, flexor pollicis longus)
- Default: "Extensor and flexor tendons are intact with normal calibre and signal. No tenosynovitis."

**Median, Ulnar, and Radial Nerves**
- Default: "Median nerve normal calibre and signal within the carpal tunnel. Ulnar nerve normal within Guyon's canal."
- If carpal tunnel syndrome: comment on median nerve flattening, palmar bowing of the flexor retinaculum, and T2 hyperintensity within the nerve.

**Articular Cartilage and Joints**
- Radiocarpal, midcarpal, distal radioulnar (DRUJ), carpometacarpal joints
- Default: "Articular cartilage intact throughout, no focal chondral defect. DRUJ congruent."

**Soft Tissues**
- Default: "No ganglion cyst. Surrounding soft tissues unremarkable."

### Impression:
**Instructions:**
- Concise bullet-point summary of key findings.
- Include only clinically relevant findings. Minor incidental findings that are fully described in the body do not need to be restated here.
- For a normal/negative study, include a closing statement where appropriate, e.g. 'No cause for the patient's symptoms identified on this examination.'
- **Sample:**
  - Scapholunate ligament tear with early SLAC changes.
  - TFCC intact. No carpal bone fracture or AVN.
