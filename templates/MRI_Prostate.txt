### Exam:
MRI Prostate (mpMRI)

### Technique:
Multiparametric MRI of the prostate was performed using a 3T scanner. Sequences included high-resolution T2-weighted in axial, coronal, and sagittal planes; diffusion-weighted imaging (DWI) with ADC mapping; and dynamic contrast-enhanced (DCE) T1-weighted sequences following intravenous gadolinium administration.

### Clinical Details
**Instructions:**
- Record clinical details exactly as dictated. Do not rephrase or reformulate the wording.
- **Exclude** patient name, date of birth, age, gender, and MRN/accession numbers from the report, even if mentioned in the transcript.
- **If not provided:** 'No clinical details provided.'

### Comparison:
**Instructions:**
- **If provided:** List the prior imaging studies mentioned (modality and date/year if stated). Do not summarise changes here — comparison findings belong in the Findings section.
- **If not provided:** 'No prior imaging available for comparison.'

### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- First, identify at what anatomical site the pathologies are from the transcript. Then format it correctly using PI-RADS v2.1 lexicon — describe lesion location (zone, sector per PI-RADS sector map), size, T2 signal, diffusion restriction, and DCE characteristics.
- First mention the structures having the pathology.
- Then mention ALL the structures that are normal. For your guidance, an mpMRI Prostate report involves Prostate (size in three dimensions, estimated volume, zonal anatomy: peripheral zone, transition zone, central zone), Prostatic Urethra, Seminal Vesicles (bilateral), Neurovascular Bundles (bilateral), Prostatic Capsule (extracapsular extension), Bladder Neck, Rectum, Pelvic Lymph Nodes, Bony Pelvis (if marrow signal abnormality), and Visualized Pelvic Structures. If not mentioned, state "{structure} appears normal."

  - Normal structures within the same anatomical group may be combined into a single statement. Only itemise individually when pathology is present.
- **Commonly Misspelled Words:**
[correct spellings] PI-RADS, multiparametric, peripheral zone, transition zone, seminal vesicles, extracapsular extension, neurovascular bundle, ADC, diffusion restriction, prostatic capsule, benign prostatic hyperplasia [correct spellings]
- If 'query' is mentioned in the transcript, it should be rendered as '?' in the report.
- Assign PI-RADS score (1–5) for each index lesion. State overall PI-RADS assessment.

### Impression:
**Instructions:**
- Create an impression from findings, into a brief description in bullet points. Include PI-RADS score.
- Include only clinically relevant findings. Minor incidental findings that are fully described in the body do not need to be restated here.
- For a normal/negative study, include a closing statement where appropriate, e.g. 'No cause for the patient's symptoms identified on this examination.'
- **Sample:**
  - Right posterior mid-peripheral zone lesion — PI-RADS 5 (clinically significant prostate cancer highly likely).
  - No evidence of extracapsular extension or seminal vesicle invasion.
