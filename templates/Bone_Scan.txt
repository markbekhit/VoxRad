### Comparison:

### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- First, identify at what anatomical site the abnormal tracer uptake is from the transcript. Then format it correctly, describing increased or decreased uptake, its distribution (focal/diffuse/superscan), and likely aetiology.
- First mention the sites of abnormal uptake.
- Then mention ALL the regions that demonstrate normal uptake. For your guidance, a Bone Scan report involves Skull and Calvarium, Facial Bones and Mandible, Cervical Spine, Thoracic Spine, Lumbar Spine, Sacrum and Coccyx, Pelvis (iliac wings, acetabulum, pubic rami, ischium), Ribs (each side — upper, mid, lower ribs), Sternum, Clavicles and Acromioclavicular Joints, Scapulae, Humeri, Elbows and Forearms, Wrists and Hands, Femora, Knees, Tibiae and Fibulae, Ankles and Feet, Soft Tissue Uptake, Kidneys and Bladder (physiological), and Renal Uptake Symmetry. If not mentioned, state "{region} demonstrates normal uptake."

  - Normal structures within the same anatomical group may be combined into a single statement. Only itemise individually when pathology is present.
- **Commonly Misspelled Words:**
[correct spellings] scintigraphy, technetium, methylene diphosphonate, tracer uptake, osteoblastic, osteolytic, superscan, photopenic, metastasis, metastases, degenerative, sacroiliac [correct spellings]
- If 'query' is mentioned in the transcript, it should be rendered as '?' in the report.

### Impression:
**Instructions:**
- Create an impression from findings, into a brief description in bullet points.
- **Sample:**

  - No superscan pattern. Kidneys demonstrate symmetric physiological uptake.
