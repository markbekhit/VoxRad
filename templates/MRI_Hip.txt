### Exam:
MRI Hip

### Technique:
MRI of the [right/left / bilateral] hip was performed using T1-weighted, T2-weighted fat-suppressed, PD-weighted fat-suppressed, and STIR sequences in coronal, axial, and sagittal planes. [If arthrogram: MR arthrography was performed following intra-articular gadolinium injection.]

### Clinical Details
**Instructions:**
- **If provided:**
  - 'The patient presented with [describe symptoms or reason for the examination], with a medical history notable for [mention relevant medical conditions].'
  - **Exclude** name, age, gender, and IDs anywhere in the report, even if provided in the transcript.

- **If not provided:**
  - 'No clinical details provided.'

### Comparison:
**Instructions:**
- **If provided:** Mention any prior imaging studies and briefly note significant changes or findings compared to previous examinations.
- **If not provided:** 'No prior imaging scans/reports available for comparison.'

### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- First, identify at what anatomical site the pathologies are from the transcript. Then format it correctly, describing morphology, signal characteristics, and degree of involvement.
- First mention the structures having the pathology.
- Then mention ALL the structures that are normal. For your guidance, an MRI Hip report involves Femoral Head (signal, sphericity, subchondral zone), Acetabulum and Acetabular Labrum (anterior, posterior, superior), Articular Cartilage (femoral head and acetabulum), Femoral Neck (angulation, osteonecrosis), Greater and Lesser Trochanters, Hip Joint Capsule and Ligamentum Teres, Iliopsoas Tendon and Bursa, Trochanteric Bursa, Gluteal Tendons (gluteus medius, minimus), Obturator Internus, Piriformis, Hamstring Origin, Sciatic Nerve, Pubic Symphysis, Sacroiliac Joints (if included), and Marrow Signal of Visualized Pelvis. If not mentioned, state "{structure} appears normal."

  - DO NOT JUST SAY "No other structures mentioned, assumed to be normal."
  - DO NOT club rest of the structures to be normal.
  - BUT RATHER mention each in a new bullet point.
- **Commonly Misspelled Words:**
[correct spellings] acetabular, labrum, femoroacetabular impingement, osteonecrosis, avascular necrosis, trochanteric, iliopsoas, gluteus medius, subchondral, cam lesion, pincer lesion [correct spellings]
- If 'query' is mentioned in the transcript, it should be rendered as '?' in the report.

### Impression:
**Instructions:**
- Create an impression from findings, into a brief description in bullet points.
- **Sample:**
  - Early avascular necrosis of the [right/left] femoral head, Ficat stage [I/II].
  - No significant labral tear or femoroacetabular impingement.

### Advice/Recommendation(s):
**Instructions:**
- Offer recommendations for further evaluation or management based on the impression of the report.
- 'Orthopaedic consultation is advised for further management of [avascular necrosis / labral tear].'
- Otherwise offer a general statement 'Clinical correlation is recommended for further evaluation and management.'
