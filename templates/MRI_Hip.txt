### Exam:
MRI Hip

### Technique:
MRI of the [right/left / bilateral] hip was performed using T1-weighted, T2-weighted fat-suppressed, PD-weighted fat-suppressed, and STIR sequences in coronal, axial, and sagittal planes. [If arthrogram: MR arthrography was performed following intra-articular gadolinium injection.]

### Clinical Details
**Instructions:**
- **If provided:**
  - 'The patient presented with [describe symptoms or reason for the examination], with a medical history notable for [mention relevant medical conditions].'
  - **Exclude** name, age, gender, and IDs anywhere in the report, even if provided in the transcript.

- **If not provided:**
  - 'No clinical details provided.'

### Comparison:
**Instructions:**
- **If provided:** Mention any prior imaging studies and briefly note significant changes or findings compared to previous examinations.
- **If not provided:** 'No prior imaging scans/reports available for comparison.'

### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- Present findings using the bold section headers below in this exact order. Do NOT create new headings or merge sections.
- For each section: if the radiologist mentioned the structure (normal or abnormal), reproduce their exact words. If the radiologist did NOT mention it, use the default normal descriptor shown below.
- Normal structures within the same section may be combined into one statement. Only itemise individually when pathology is present.

**Femoral Head and Neck**
- Default: "Femoral head: normal morphology and signal. No avascular necrosis, subchondral fracture, or cam deformity. Femoral neck: normal alignment and signal."
- Describe alpha angle if cam morphology is mentioned.

**Acetabulum and Labrum**
- Default: "Acetabulum: normal morphology. No pincer deformity. Labrum intact, no tear or paralabral cyst."
- If pathology: report labral tear by compartment (anterior, posterior, superior, inferior); describe chondrolabral junction involvement if present.

**Articular Cartilage**
- Default: "Hip joint articular cartilage intact. No focal chondral defect."
- If pathology: describe by surface (femoral head / acetabular) and extent.

**Hip Joint**
- Default: "No significant hip joint effusion. Joint capsule unremarkable. Ligamentum teres intact."

**Gluteal Tendons**
- Default: "Gluteus medius and gluteus minimus tendons intact at their greater trochanteric insertion. No tendinopathy or tear."

**Iliopsoas Tendon and Bursa**
- Default: "Iliopsoas tendon intact. No iliopsoas bursitis."

**Hamstring Origin**
- Default: "Hamstring tendons intact at the ischial tuberosity. No avulsion or tendinopathy."

**Trochanteric Bursa**
- Default: "No trochanteric bursitis."

**Pelvic Structures**
- Default: "Pubic symphysis unremarkable. Sacroiliac joints unremarkable (to the extent visualised). Sciatic nerve: normal calibre, no compression."

**Bony Structures**
- ALWAYS use the radiologist's exact dictated words for bony/marrow findings, whether normal or abnormal. Examples:
  - If they said "no fracture, no marrow oedema" → write exactly that.
  - If they said "insufficiency fracture of the femoral neck" → write exactly that.
- Only use the default "Marrow signal normal throughout the visualised pelvis and proximal femora. No fracture or aggressive bony lesion." if the radiologist said NOTHING about bony structures.
- If a specific abnormality is described, state it, then add "Remaining marrow signal is normal."
