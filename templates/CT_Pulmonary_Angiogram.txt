### Exam:
CT Pulmonary Angiogram (CTPA)

### Technique:
Axial CT images of the chest were acquired during peak pulmonary arterial enhancement following intravenous contrast administration using a pulmonary embolism protocol. Coronal and sagittal reformats and thin-slice MIPs were reviewed.

### Clinical Details
**Instructions:**
- Record clinical details exactly as dictated. Do not rephrase or reformulate the wording.
- **Exclude** patient name, date of birth, age, gender, and MRN/accession numbers from the report, even if mentioned in the transcript.
- **If not provided:** 'No clinical details provided.'

### Comparison:
**Instructions:**
- **If provided:** List the prior imaging studies mentioned (modality and date/year if stated). Do not summarise changes here — comparison findings belong in the Findings section.
- **If not provided:** 'No prior imaging available for comparison.'

### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- Present findings using the bold section headers below in this exact order. Do NOT create new headings or rename sections.
- For each section: if the radiologist mentioned it, reproduce their exact words. If not mentioned, use an appropriate normal descriptor — do NOT write "appears normal" generically; use precise radiology language.
- The Pulmonary Arteries section must always be addressed first and explicitly — it is the clinical question.

**Contrast Opacification**
- Default: "Diagnostic-quality pulmonary arterial opacification."
- If suboptimal: state the reason (timing, motion, respiratory) and the level of the arterial tree that remains diagnostic.

**Pulmonary Arteries**
- Default: "No filling defect in the main, lobar, segmental, or visualised subsegmental pulmonary arteries. No acute pulmonary embolism."
- If positive: describe clot burden (unilateral/bilateral, central [main/lobar] vs peripheral [segmental/subsegmental]), specific vessels involved, and whether acute or chronic features are present.

**Right Heart Strain (if PE present)**
- Comment on RV:LV diameter ratio, septal bowing, reflux of contrast into the IVC / hepatic veins, and pulmonary artery calibre (main PA > 29 mm suggests pulmonary hypertension).

**Lungs**
- Default: "Lungs clear. No consolidation, infarct, or nodule."
- Look specifically for wedge-shaped peripheral opacities (pulmonary infarcts) when PE is present.

**Pleura**
- Default: "No pleural effusion or pneumothorax."

**Mediastinum and Hila**
- Default: "Mediastinum unremarkable. No mediastinal or hilar lymphadenopathy. No mediastinal mass."

**Heart and Pericardium**
- Default: "Heart normal in size. No pericardial effusion."

**Thoracic Aorta and Great Vessels**
- Default: "Thoracic aorta normal in calibre and contour. No aortic dissection. No acute aortic syndrome."

**Chest Wall and Soft Tissues**
- Default: "Chest wall and soft tissues unremarkable."

**Bony Thorax**
- ALWAYS use the radiologist's exact dictated words for bony findings. Only use the default "Bony thorax intact. No lytic or sclerotic lesion." if the radiologist said NOTHING about bones.

**Visualised Upper Abdomen**
- Default: "Visualised upper abdominal structures unremarkable."

- If 'query' is mentioned in the transcript, it should be rendered as '?' in the report.

### Impression:
**Instructions:**
- Concise bullet-point summary. The first bullet must explicitly address PE (present or absent).
- Include only clinically relevant findings. Minor incidental findings that are fully described in the body do not need to be restated here.
- For a normal/negative study, include a closing statement where appropriate, e.g. 'No pulmonary embolism or alternative cause for the patient's symptoms identified on this examination.'
- **Sample:**
  - No acute pulmonary embolism.
  - No alternative acute cardiopulmonary abnormality identified.
