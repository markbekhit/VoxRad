### Exam:
CT Abdomen and Pelvis

### Technique:
Axial cuts were obtained from the dome of the diaphragm to the pubic symphysis. Coronal and sagittal reformatting were then obtained. [If contrast: Scans were acquired in the [pre-contrast / arterial / portal venous / delayed] phase following intravenous contrast administration.]

### Clinical Details
**Instructions:**
- **If provided:**
  - 'The patient presented with [describe symptoms or reason for the examination], with a medical history notable for [mention relevant medical conditions].'
  - Offer a concise overview of the patient's relevant medical history and the reason for the examination. Include details such as symptoms, pertinent clinical history, and any known medical conditions.
  - **Exclude** name, age, gender, and IDs anywhere in the report, even if provided in the transcript.

- **If not provided:**
  - 'No clinical details provided.'

### Comparison:
**Instructions:**
- **If provided:** Mention any prior imaging studies and briefly note significant changes or findings compared to previous examinations.
- **If not provided:** 'No prior imaging scans/reports available for comparison.'

### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- First, identify at what anatomical site the pathologies are from the transcript. Then format it correctly, describing its morphology, and if the lesion is a mass, mention its extension and relations.
- First mention the structures having the pathology.
- Then mention ALL the structures that are normal. Use your intelligence to carefully think about what all structures are visualized in a CT Abdomen and Pelvis. For your guidance, a CT Abdomen and Pelvis report involves regions like Liver, Gallbladder and Biliary System, Spleen, Pancreas, Kidneys and Ureters, Adrenal Glands, Bowel (Small Bowel and Large Bowel), Mesentery and Omentum, Peritoneum and Ascites, Abdominal Aorta and IVC, Lymph Nodes, Urinary Bladder, Prostate/Uterus and Ovaries (if visualized), Rectum, Pelvic Sidewalls, and Visualized Bony Pelvis and Lumbar Spine. If the transcript doesn't mention anything about a certain structure, mention that "{the organ} appears normal."

  - Normal structures within the same anatomical group may be combined into a single statement. Only itemise individually when pathology is present.
- Do not create unnecessary sub-bullets for single words or phrases. Instead, use complete sentences to describe each finding.
- **Consolidation of Similar Findings:** Assess the transcript for mentions of similar radiological findings located in different locations. These similar findings should be consolidated into a single, comprehensive statement.
- **Commonly Misspelled Words:** Pay special attention to commonly misspelled words in transcription such as:
[correct spellings] hepatomegaly, splenomegaly, cholelithiasis, hydronephrosis, nephrolithiasis, pneumoperitoneum, retroperitoneal, mesenteric, peritoneal, intussusception, diverticulum, appendicitis, iliopsoas [correct spellings]
- If 'query' is mentioned in the transcript, it should be rendered as '?' in the report.

### Impression:
**Instructions:**
- Create an impression from findings, into a brief description in bullet points. Provide an overall findings are suggestive of differential diagnosis or final diagnosis.
- **Sample:**
  - No significant abnormality identified in the abdomen and pelvis.
  - *Overall, the imaging findings are within normal limits.*
