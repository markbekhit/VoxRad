### THIS IS THE SAMPLE TEMPLATE TO BE USED FOR THE TRANSCRIPT ###

---

### Exam:
CT Chest

### Technique:
Axial cuts were obtained from the thoracic inlet to the upper abdomen. Coronal and sagittal reformatting were then obtained. [If contrast: Scans were acquired following intravenous contrast administration.]

### Clinical Details
**Instructions:**
- Record clinical details exactly as dictated. Do not rephrase or reformulate the wording.
- **Exclude** patient name, date of birth, age, gender, and MRN/accession numbers from the report, even if mentioned in the transcript.
- **If not provided:** 'No clinical details provided.'

### Comparison:
**Instructions:**
- **If provided:** List the prior imaging studies mentioned (modality and date/year if stated). Do not summarise changes here — comparison findings belong in the Findings section.
- **If not provided:** 'No prior imaging available for comparison.'

### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- Present findings using the bold section headers below in this exact order. Do NOT create new headings or rename sections.
- For each section: if the radiologist mentioned it, reproduce their exact words. If not mentioned, use an appropriate normal descriptor — do NOT write "appears normal" generically; use precise radiology language.
- Normal groups may be combined into a single statement. Only itemise individually when pathology is present.
- Do not create sub-bullets for single words or phrases — use complete sentences.

**Lungs**
- Default: "Lungs clear. No consolidation, collapse, nodule, or mass. No bronchiectasis."
- If pathology: describe by lobe and segment with morphology (size, density in HU if relevant, distribution).

**Airways**
- Default: "Airways patent to the segmental level. No endobronchial lesion or tracheal deviation."

**Pleura**
- Default: "No pleural effusion, pneumothorax, or pleural thickening."

**Mediastinum**
- Default: "Mediastinum unremarkable. No mediastinal mass or lymphadenopathy."
- Describe lymph nodes by station (subcarinal, paratracheal, aortopulmonary window) when enlarged (short axis > 10 mm).

**Hila**
- Default: "Hila unremarkable. No hilar lymphadenopathy or mass."

**Heart and Pericardium**
- Default: "Heart normal in size. No pericardial effusion."

**Thoracic Aorta and Great Vessels**
- Default: "Thoracic aorta normal in calibre and contour. Great vessels unremarkable."

**Axillary Regions**
- Default: "No axillary lymphadenopathy."

**Chest Wall and Soft Tissues**
- Default: "Chest wall and soft tissues unremarkable."

**Bony Thorax**
- ALWAYS use the radiologist's exact dictated words for bony findings. Only use the default "Bony thorax intact. No lytic or sclerotic lesion." if the radiologist said NOTHING about bones.

**Visualised Upper Abdomen**
- Default: "Visualised upper abdominal structures unremarkable."

Lines, tubes, and devices — comment if present.

- **ASR Prompt (for speech recognition spelling correction ONLY — do NOT copy this text into the report):**
[correct spellings] CT chest with contrast. The lungs are clear with no consolidation, atelectasis, or pneumothorax. No pleural effusion. No pulmonary embolism. Airways patent to the segmental level. No bronchiectasis. Mediastinum unremarkable. No mediastinal or hilar lymphadenopathy. No subcarinal lymphadenopathy. Heart normal in size. No pericardial effusion. Thoracic aorta normal calibre. No axillary lymphadenopathy. Bony thorax intact, no lytic or sclerotic lesion. [correct spellings]
- If 'query' is mentioned in the transcript, it should be rendered as '?' in the report.

### Impression:
**Instructions:**
- Concise bullet-point summary of key findings.
- Include only clinically relevant findings. Minor incidental findings that are fully described in the body do not need to be restated here.
- For a normal/negative study, include a closing statement where appropriate, e.g. 'No cause for the patient's symptoms identified on this examination.'
- **Sample:**
  - No significant abnormality identified in the chest.
