### THIS IS THE SAMPLE TEMPLATE TO BE USED FOR THE TRANSCRIPT ###

---

### Exam:
Ultrasound Breast

### Technique:
Ultrasound examination of [the right / the left / both] breast(s) was performed using a high-frequency linear transducer. Systematic evaluation of all quadrants and the retroareolar region was performed. [If targeted: Directed ultrasound was performed to correlate with the [clinical / mammographic / MRI] finding.]

### Clinical Details
**Instructions:**
- Record clinical details exactly as dictated. Do not rephrase or reformulate the wording.
- **Exclude** patient name, date of birth, age, gender, and MRN/accession numbers from the report, even if mentioned in the transcript.
- **If not provided:** 'No clinical details provided.'

### Comparison:
**Instructions:**
- **If provided:** List the prior imaging studies mentioned (modality and date/year if stated). Do not summarise changes here — comparison findings belong in the Findings section.
- **If not provided:** 'No prior imaging available for comparison.'

### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- First, identify at what anatomical site the pathologies are from the transcript. Then format it correctly using ACR BI-RADS lexicon — describe mass shape (oval/round/irregular), orientation (parallel/not parallel), margin (circumscribed/indistinct/angular/microlobulated/spiculated), and echo pattern (anechoic/hyperechoic/complex/hypoechoic/isoechoic/heterogeneous).
- First mention the structures having the pathology.
- Then mention ALL the structures that are normal. For your guidance, a Breast Ultrasound report involves Right Breast (each quadrant and clock position, retroareolar region, skin and subcutaneous fat), Left Breast, Nipple–Areolar Complex (bilateral), Right Axilla and Axillary Lymph Nodes, Left Axilla and Axillary Lymph Nodes, and Tissue Composition (fatty / fibroglandular). If not mentioned, state "{structure} appears normal."

  - Normal structures within the same anatomical group may be combined into a single statement. Only itemise individually when pathology is present.
- **Commonly Misspelled Words:**
[correct spellings] BI-RADS, hypoechoic, hyperechoic, anechoic, circumscribed, spiculated, microlobulated, posterior acoustic enhancement, posterior shadowing, retroareolar, axillary, lymphadenopathy [correct spellings]
- If 'query' is mentioned in the transcript, it should be rendered as '?' in the report.
- Assign a final BI-RADS category (0–6) in the impression.

### Impression:
**Instructions:**
- Create an impression from findings, into a brief description in bullet points. Include BI-RADS category.
- Include only clinically relevant findings. Minor incidental findings that are fully described in the body do not need to be restated here.
- For a normal/negative study, include a closing statement where appropriate, e.g. 'No cause for the patient's symptoms identified on this examination.'
- **Sample:**
  - Right breast 2 o'clock oval circumscribed hypoechoic mass — BI-RADS 3 (probably benign). Short-term follow-up in 6 months.
  - No suspicious lesion in the left breast.
