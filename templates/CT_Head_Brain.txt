### THIS IS CONTINUATION OF SYSTEM PROMPT ###
### THIS IS THE SAMPLE TEMPLATE TO BE USED FOR THE TRANSCRIPT ###

---

### Exam:
CT Head and Brain

### Technique:
Axial cuts were obtained from the skull base to the vertex. Bone window and soft tissue window reconstructions were reviewed. [If contrast: Scans were acquired pre- and post-intravenous contrast administration.]

### Clinical Details
**Instructions:**
- **If provided:**
  - 'The patient presented with [describe symptoms or reason for the examination], with a medical history notable for [mention relevant medical conditions].'
  - Offer a concise overview of the patient's relevant medical history and the reason for the examination. Include details such as symptoms, pertinent clinical history, and any known medical conditions.
  - **Exclude** name, age, gender, and IDs anywhere in the report, even if provided in the transcript.

- **If not provided:**
  - 'No clinical details provided.'

### Comparison:
**Instructions:**
- **If provided:** Mention any prior imaging studies and briefly note significant changes or findings compared to previous examinations.
- **If not provided:** 'No prior imaging scans/reports available for comparison.'

### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- First, identify at what anatomical site the pathologies are from the transcript. Then format it correctly, describing its morphology, and if the lesion is a mass, mention its extension and relations.
- First mention the structures having the pathology.
- Then mention ALL the structures that are normal. Use your intelligence to carefully think about what all structures are visualized in a CT Head. For your guidance, a CT Head report involves regions like Brain Parenchyma (cerebral hemispheres, cerebellum, brainstem), White Matter, Basal Ganglia, Thalami, Internal Capsules, Ventricles (lateral, third, fourth), Sulci and Gyri, Basal Cisterns, Corpus Callosum, Cerebellopontine Angles, Sella and Parasellar Region, Skull Vault and Calvarium, Paranasal Sinuses, Mastoid Air Cells, Orbits (if included), and Extracranial Soft Tissues. If the transcript doesn't mention anything about a certain structure, mention that "{the structure} appears normal."

  - DO NOT JUST SAY "No other structures mentioned, assumed to be normal."
  - DO NOT club rest of the structures to be normal.
  - BUT RATHER mention each in a new bullet point.
    For instance:
    - **Brain Parenchyma:** No focal hypodense or hyperdense lesion identified.
    - **Ventricles:** Normal in size and configuration.
    - **Basal Ganglia and Thalami:** Normal.
    - and so on...
- Do not create unnecessary sub-bullets for single words or phrases. Instead, use complete sentences to describe each finding.
- **Consolidation of Similar Findings:** Consolidate similar findings across different locations into a single comprehensive statement.
- **Commonly Misspelled Words:** Pay special attention to commonly misspelled words in transcription such as:
[correct spellings] cerebral, cerebellar, subarachnoid, subdural, extradural, haemorrhage, infarction, periventricular, leukoaraiosis, hydrocephalus, gyri, sulci, cisterns, tentorial, uncal herniation, cerebellopontine [correct spellings]
- If 'query' is mentioned in the transcript, it should be rendered as '?' in the report.

### Impression:
**Instructions:**
- Create an impression from findings, into a brief description in bullet points. Provide an overall findings are suggestive of differential diagnosis or final diagnosis.
- **Sample:**
  - No intracranial haemorrhage, mass lesion, or midline shift identified.
  - *Overall, the CT head is within normal limits.*

### Advice/Recommendation(s):
**Instructions:**
- Offer recommendations for further evaluation or management based on the impression of the report.
- **Sample Descriptive Style:**
  - 'MRI brain with contrast is recommended for further characterisation of [specific finding].'
  - 'Neurosurgical consultation is advised for appropriate management.'
  - If no significant findings are identified, state 'No further radiological workup required.'
