### Exam:
Ultrasound Abdomen

### Technique:
Ultrasound examination of the abdomen was performed using a curvilinear transducer. Real-time grayscale and colour Doppler interrogation of the abdominal organs and vasculature were performed.

### Clinical Details
**Instructions:**
- **If provided:**
  - 'The patient presented with [describe symptoms or reason for the examination], with a medical history notable for [mention relevant medical conditions].'
  - **Exclude** name, age, gender, and IDs anywhere in the report, even if provided in the transcript.

- **If not provided:**
  - 'No clinical details provided.'

### Comparison:
**Instructions:**
- **If provided:** Mention any prior imaging studies and briefly note significant changes or findings compared to previous examinations.
- **If not provided:** 'No prior imaging scans/reports available for comparison.'

### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- First, identify at what anatomical site the pathologies are from the transcript. Then format it correctly, describing echogenicity, size, and morphology of lesions.
- First mention the structures having the pathology.
- Then mention ALL the structures that are normal. For your guidance, an Ultrasound Abdomen report involves Liver (size, echogenicity, surface, focal lesions), Gallbladder (size, wall, calculi, polyps, CBD diameter), Common Bile Duct, Spleen (size, echogenicity), Pancreas (head, body, tail, main pancreatic duct — if visualised), Right Kidney (size, cortical thickness, echogenicity, collecting system, calculi), Left Kidney, Urinary Bladder (if filled), Aorta and IVC (calibre, aneurysm), Free Fluid (ascites), Lymph Nodes (para-aortic, coeliac), and Visualized Bowel. If not mentioned, state "{structure} appears normal/not visualised."

  - DO NOT JUST SAY "No other structures mentioned, assumed to be normal."
  - DO NOT club rest of the structures to be normal.
  - BUT RATHER mention each in a new bullet point.
    For instance:
    - **Liver:** Mildly enlarged liver (craniocaudal span [X] cm) with diffusely increased echogenicity consistent with hepatic steatosis.
    - **Gallbladder:** Multiple echogenic foci with posterior acoustic shadowing, in keeping with cholelithiasis. No wall thickening or pericholecystic fluid.
    - **Common Bile Duct:** Not dilated ([X] mm).
    - and so on...
- **Commonly Misspelled Words:**
[correct spellings] echogenicity, echogenic, hypoechoic, hyperechoic, anechoic, hepatomegaly, splenomegaly, cholelithiasis, cholecystitis, nephrolithiasis, hydronephrosis, acoustic shadowing, posterior enhancement, hepatic steatosis [correct spellings]
- If 'query' is mentioned in the transcript, it should be rendered as '?' in the report.

### Impression:
**Instructions:**
- Create an impression from findings, into a brief description in bullet points.
- **Sample:**
  - Hepatic steatosis with cholelithiasis.
  - No intrahepatic biliary dilatation. CBD not dilated.
  - Normal bilateral kidneys.
