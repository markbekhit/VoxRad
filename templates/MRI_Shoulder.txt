### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- First, identify at what anatomical site the pathologies are from the transcript. Then format it correctly, describing morphology, signal, and extent of tear (partial/full-thickness, retraction).
- First mention the structures having the pathology specifically. Then cover remaining structures, grouping normals within the same anatomical category rather than forcing a separate bullet for every structure.
- For your guidance, an MRI Shoulder report covers: Rotator Cuff, Long Head of Biceps Tendon, Glenohumeral Labrum, Glenohumeral Articular Cartilage, Subacromial–Subdeltoid Bursa, Acromioclavicular Joint, Acromion, Coracoclavicular Ligaments, Bony Structures, Deltoid Muscle, and Axillary Recess.

  Grouping guidance:
  - **Rotator Cuff:** If only one tendon is abnormal, describe it specifically, then state the remaining tendons (by name) as intact in a single line. e.g. "Infraspinatus, teres minor, and subscapularis: intact."  If all four are normal: "Rotator cuff tendons (supraspinatus, infraspinatus, teres minor, subscapularis): intact, no tear."
  - **Labrum:** Report by compartment (anterior, posterior, superior/SLAP) only if pathology is present; otherwise "Labrum intact."
  - Other structures may be grouped if all normal within the same category.
