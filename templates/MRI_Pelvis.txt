MRI Pelvis

MRI of the pelvis was performed using T2-weighted, T1-weighted, DWI/ADC, and dynamic contrast-enhanced sequences in axial, coronal, and sagittal planes. High-resolution T2-weighted sequences were obtained in orthogonal planes relative to the organ of interest.

### Clinical Details
**Instructions:**

- **Exclude** patient name, date of birth, age, gender, and MRN/accession numbers from the report, even if mentioned in the transcript.
- **If not provided:** 'No clinical details provided.'

### Comparison:
**Instructions:**

### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- First, identify at what anatomical site the pathologies are from the transcript. Then format it correctly.
- First mention the structures having the pathology.
- Then mention ALL the structures that are normal. For your guidance, an MRI Pelvis report involves Uterus (zonal anatomy: endometrium, junctional zone, myometrium), Cervix, Vagina, Ovaries (size, follicles, cysts, masses), Adnexae, Fallopian Tubes (if visible), Urinary Bladder (wall, lumen), Rectum and Sigmoid Colon, Small Bowel (if visualised), Peritoneum and Ascites, Pelvic Lymph Nodes, Pelvic Sidewalls and Muscles (levator ani, obturator internus, piriformis), Sacrum and Coccyx, Pubic Symphysis, Iliac Vessels, and Inguinal Regions. [In male patients, replace with: Prostate, Seminal Vesicles, Vas Deferens, Testes (if included), Penis (if included).] If not mentioned, state "{structure} appears normal."

  - Normal structures within the same anatomical group may be combined into a single statement. Only itemise individually when pathology is present.

### Impression:
**Instructions:**
- Create an impression from findings, into a brief description in bullet points.
- Include only clinically relevant findings. Minor incidental findings that are fully described in the body do not need to be restated here.
- For a normal/negative study, include a closing statement where appropriate, e.g. 'No cause for the patient's symptoms identified on this examination.'
- **Sample:**
  - Uterine fibroid(s) as described — no malignant features.
  - No adnexal mass or free fluid.
