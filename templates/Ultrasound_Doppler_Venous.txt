### Exam:
Doppler Ultrasound — Lower Limb Veins (DVT Assessment)

### Technique:
Greyscale, colour, and spectral Doppler ultrasound of the [right/left/bilateral] lower limb deep venous system was performed. Compressibility, colour filling, spontaneous and augmented flow, and phasicity with respiration were assessed at each segment.

### Clinical Details
**Instructions:**
- Record clinical details exactly as dictated. Do not rephrase or reformulate the wording.
- **Exclude** patient name, date of birth, age, gender, and MRN/accession numbers from the report, even if mentioned in the transcript.
- **If not provided:** 'No clinical details provided.'

### Comparison:
**Instructions:**
- **If provided:** List the prior imaging studies mentioned (modality and date/year if stated). Do not summarise changes here — comparison findings belong in the Findings section.
- **If not provided:** 'No prior imaging available for comparison.'

### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- Present findings using the bold section headers below in this exact order. Do NOT create new headings or rename sections.
- For EACH segment explicitly state: (i) compressibility, (ii) colour filling, and (iii) flow characteristics. Do not collapse the report into a single sentence — the clinical question (thrombus presence and extent) requires a segment-by-segment assessment.
- If bilateral: repeat the structure for both sides. If unilateral: include only the side studied.

**Common Femoral Vein**
- Default: "Fully compressible. Normal colour filling, phasic flow with respiration, and augmentation on distal compression. No intraluminal thrombus."

**Common Femoral Vein / Saphenofemoral Junction**
- Default: "Normal. No thrombus extending into the great saphenous vein."

**Femoral Vein (proximal, mid, and distal / adductor canal)**
- Default: "Fully compressible throughout. Normal colour filling and phasic flow. No intraluminal thrombus."

**Popliteal Vein**
- Default: "Fully compressible. Normal colour filling and augmentation. No intraluminal thrombus."

**Calf Veins (Posterior Tibial, Peroneal, Gastrocnemius, Soleal)**
- Default: "Paired posterior tibial and peroneal veins are compressible with normal colour filling. Gastrocnemius and soleal venous sinuses are unremarkable."
- If not fully examined (e.g., limited by body habitus or pain): explicitly state the limitation.

**Superficial Veins (Great and Short Saphenous)**
- Default: "Great and short saphenous veins are patent with normal calibre. No superficial thrombophlebitis."

**Other**
- Comment on any Baker's cyst, haematoma, or soft-tissue collection encountered that could explain the clinical presentation.

### Impression:
**Instructions:**
- Concise bullet-point summary. The first bullet must explicitly address the presence or absence of DVT and its level (proximal vs isolated distal).
- Include only clinically relevant findings. Minor incidental findings that are fully described in the body do not need to be restated here.
- For a normal/negative study, include a closing statement where appropriate, e.g. 'No evidence of deep venous thrombosis on this examination.'
- **Sample:**
  - No evidence of acute deep venous thrombosis in the [right/left/bilateral] lower limb(s).
  - Examined veins are patent and fully compressible with normal phasic flow.
