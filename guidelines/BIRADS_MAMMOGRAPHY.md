# ACR BI-RADS Mammography — 5th Edition

The Breast Imaging Reporting and Data System (BI-RADS) standardises
mammographic reporting and the resulting management. The final BI-RADS
category drives the recommendation; never give vague phrasing like
"consider biopsy" or "follow-up as needed."

## Categories and Management Actions

### Category 0 — Incomplete: needs additional imaging
- **Findings**: One-view-only abnormality, asymmetry without confirmed
  margins, calcifications needing magnification views, or technical
  inadequacy.
- **Action**: Diagnostic mammography (spot compression, magnification,
  rolled / true-lateral views) and/or targeted ultrasound. Use Cat 0
  ONLY for screening — once diagnostic workup is complete, assign a
  final 1-5 category.

### Category 1 — Negative
- **Findings**: No mammographic abnormality.
- **Action**: Routine screening — annual mammography from 40 (per
  RANZCR / BreastScreen Australia) or per national programme schedule.

### Category 2 — Benign
- **Findings**: Definitively benign — calcified fibroadenoma, secretory
  calcifications, vascular calcifications, simple cyst confirmed on US,
  intramammary lymph node, oil cyst, hamartoma, lipoma, fat necrosis.
- **Action**: Routine screening at the standard interval. State the
  benign diagnosis explicitly so the referrer does not over-investigate.

### Category 3 — Probably benign (≤ 2 % malignancy risk)
- **Findings**: Non-palpable circumscribed mass, focal asymmetry that
  appears to be a summation, or grouped punctate / round calcifications,
  established as new or not previously documented.
- **Action — full short-interval pathway**:
  1. Unilateral diagnostic mammogram ± ultrasound at **6 months**.
  2. If stable, bilateral mammogram at **12 months** from baseline.
  3. If stable, bilateral mammogram at **24 months** from baseline.
  4. After 2-3 years of stability, downgrade to BI-RADS 2.
- Do not assign Cat 3 to a palpable lesion or to a finding without prior
  diagnostic workup.

### Category 4 — Suspicious (subdivided 4A / 4B / 4C)
- **4A**: Low suspicion for malignancy (> 2 % to ≤ 10 %).
  *Findings*: Round mass with indistinct margins; new solitary group of
  punctate calcifications; palpable mass with circumscribed but not
  fully assessable margins.
- **4B**: Moderate suspicion (> 10 % to ≤ 50 %).
  *Findings*: Group of fine pleomorphic calcifications; partially
  indistinct mass with mixed margins.
- **4C**: High suspicion (> 50 % to < 95 %).
  *Findings*: New cluster of fine linear / fine-linear branching
  calcifications; irregular, indistinct, or microlobulated mass.
- **Action**: Image-guided core needle biopsy (stereotactic for
  calcifications, US-guided for masses, MRI-guided if MRI-only). After
  biopsy, follow the radiology-pathology concordance pathway.

### Category 5 — Highly suggestive of malignancy (≥ 95 %)
- **Findings**: Spiculated high-density mass, fine-linear or fine-linear
  branching calcifications, architectural distortion with associated
  spiculated mass, classic skin / nipple involvement.
- **Action**: Image-guided core biopsy as the next step, then surgical
  / oncology referral with pre-treatment staging. Sentinel lymph node
  biopsy is part of the surgical pathway, not a radiology recommendation.

### Category 6 — Known biopsy-proven malignancy
- **Findings**: Mammogram performed for treatment planning or
  monitoring of biopsy-confirmed cancer.
- **Action**: Per oncology pathway — neoadjuvant response assessment,
  surgical planning, or post-treatment surveillance.

## Calcification Distributions and Their Implications

Distribution combined with morphology drives the category:
- **Diffuse / scattered**: Almost always benign (Cat 2).
- **Regional**: Lower probability of malignancy than clustered or
  segmental.
- **Grouped (clustered)**: Few calcifications in a small volume —
  category depends on morphology.
- **Linear**: Higher probability of malignancy (ductal distribution).
- **Segmental**: Highest probability when combined with suspicious
  morphology.

## Calcification Morphology

- **Typically benign**: Skin, vascular, popcorn, large rod-like, round,
  rim, dystrophic, milk of calcium.
- **Suspicious**: Amorphous (Cat 4B), coarse heterogeneous (Cat 4B),
  fine pleomorphic (Cat 4B), fine linear / fine-linear branching
  (Cat 4C-5).

## Architectural Distortion

Distortion without a definite mass on a screening mammogram is at least
**Cat 4** if no benign cause is identified (post-surgical scar, fat
necrosis with prior trauma). Tomosynthesis-detected distortion that is
real on tomo but invisible on 2D still requires biopsy unless a clear
benign explanation exists.

## Breast Density (separate from BI-RADS category)

Reported as a, b, c, or d:
- **a**: Almost entirely fatty.
- **b**: Scattered fibroglandular density.
- **c**: Heterogeneously dense (may obscure small masses).
- **d**: Extremely dense.
Densities c and d may justify supplemental ultrasound or MRI in the
right clinical context.

## Worked Examples

**Example 1**: Screening mammogram. New 8 mm circumscribed mass in the
right upper outer quadrant, no prior comparison.
→ Need additional imaging first = **Cat 0**.
→ **Action**: Diagnostic mammogram with spot compression + targeted
ultrasound to confirm margins and characterise as solid vs cystic.

**Example 2**: Diagnostic mammogram and US confirm a 7 mm circumscribed
oval mass with parallel orientation, no internal vascularity, no prior
imaging.
→ Probably benign solid mass = **Cat 3**.
→ **Action**: Unilateral diagnostic mammogram ± US at 6 months, then
12 months from baseline, then 24 months from baseline. Downgrade to
Cat 2 after 2-3 years of stability.

**Example 3**: Cluster of fine pleomorphic calcifications in the right
upper outer quadrant.
→ Fine pleomorphic + clustered = **Cat 4B**.
→ **Action**: Stereotactic core needle biopsy. Recommend
radiology-pathology correlation after results.

**Example 4**: Spiculated high-density 22 mm mass with fine-linear
branching calcifications and associated architectural distortion.
→ Multiple high-suspicion features = **Cat 5**.
→ **Action**: US-guided or stereotactic core biopsy followed by
surgical / oncology referral with pre-treatment staging.

## Output Requirements for Impressions

When applying BI-RADS to mammography:
1. Assign exactly one final BI-RADS category per breast (or per
   abnormality if multiple).
2. State the specific action — diagnostic workup, screening interval
   (with interval named), short-interval follow-up pathway (with the
   6/12/24-month sequence), core biopsy (with modality), or oncology
   referral. Never use "consider follow-up" or "further evaluation."
3. For Cat 3, the action MUST name the full 6-12-24 month pathway, not
   just "6-month follow-up."
4. For Cat 4, name the 4A/4B/4C subcategory if assessable.
5. Report breast density (a-d) separately from the BI-RADS category.
