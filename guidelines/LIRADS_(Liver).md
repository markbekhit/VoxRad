# LI-RADS v2018 — CT/MRI Diagnostic Algorithm for HCC

The American College of Radiology Liver Imaging Reporting and Data System
(LI-RADS) v2018 is used **only in patients at risk for HCC**: cirrhosis
of any cause, chronic hepatitis B (without cirrhosis), or current/prior
HCC. Do NOT apply LI-RADS in patients without these risk factors —
observations in non-at-risk patients are characterised under standard
liver imaging principles, not LI-RADS.

## Categories

- **LR-1**: Definitely benign.
- **LR-2**: Probably benign.
- **LR-3**: Intermediate probability of malignancy.
- **LR-4**: Probably HCC.
- **LR-5**: Definitely HCC (imaging diagnosis — no biopsy required).
- **LR-M**: Probably or definitely malignant, not HCC-specific
  (e.g. cholangiocarcinoma, combined HCC-CCA).
- **LR-TIV**: Definite tumour in vein.
- **LR-NC**: Not categorisable (image quality / technique inadequate).

## Major Imaging Features (CT/MRI with extracellular agent)

A LI-RADS category for an observation is determined by:
1. **Size** (longest outer diameter, mm).
2. **Non-rim arterial phase hyperenhancement (APHE)**.
3. **Non-peripheral washout** on portal-venous or delayed phases.
4. **Enhancing capsule**.
5. **Threshold growth** (≥50% increase in diameter in ≤6 months).

For hepatobiliary contrast agents (gadoxetate), the algorithm is the same
except hepatobiliary phase hypointensity may serve as an ancillary
favouring malignancy.

## LR-5 Criteria (definitive HCC by imaging)

LR-5 is assigned when ALL of the following are met:

- Observation arises in an at-risk patient.
- Non-rim APHE present.
- ≥ 10 mm AND any one of: non-peripheral washout, enhancing capsule,
  threshold growth.
- ≥ 20 mm AND ANY of: non-peripheral washout, enhancing capsule,
  threshold growth, OR
- ≥ 20 mm with non-rim APHE only (no other features).

## LR-4 Criteria (probably HCC)

Observations meeting some but not all LR-5 criteria. Common patterns:
- 10-19 mm with non-rim APHE only.
- < 10 mm with non-rim APHE plus washout or enhancing capsule or
  threshold growth.
- 10-19 mm without non-rim APHE but with two or more of: washout,
  capsule, threshold growth.

## LR-3 Criteria (intermediate)

Lower probability than LR-4 — typically:
- < 20 mm without non-rim APHE and with at most one of washout, capsule,
  or threshold growth.
- 10-19 mm with non-rim APHE alone (when not meeting LR-4 criteria for
  20 mm threshold).

## LR-M Criteria

Probably or definitely malignant but with features atypical for HCC,
including:
- Targetoid appearance (rim APHE, peripheral washout, delayed central
  enhancement).
- Marked diffusion restriction with infiltrative pattern.
- Necrosis / severe ischaemia.
- These suggest cholangiocarcinoma or combined HCC-CCA — biopsy is
  required for definitive diagnosis.

## Management Recommendations

### LR-1 (Definitely benign)
- **Action**: Return to routine HCC surveillance (US ± AFP every 6
  months for cirrhotic patients).

### LR-2 (Probably benign)
- **Action**: Return to routine HCC surveillance every 6 months.

### LR-3 (Intermediate probability)
- **Action**: Repeat or alternative diagnostic imaging in 3-6 months.
  Multidisciplinary discussion if persistent or growing on follow-up.
- Stable LR-3 over 2 years may be downgraded to LR-2.

### LR-4 (Probably HCC)
- **Action**: Multidisciplinary discussion. Options include repeat or
  alternative imaging, biopsy, or treatment without biopsy depending on
  clinical context (transplant candidacy, lesion accessibility).

### LR-5 (Definitely HCC)
- **Action**: HCC management pathway (transplant evaluation, resection,
  loco-regional therapy such as TACE / TARE / ablation, or systemic
  therapy depending on stage).
- **Biopsy is NOT required** — LR-5 is the definitive imaging diagnosis
  of HCC under AASLD and EASL guidelines. Do not recommend biopsy unless
  there is diagnostic uncertainty or the management pathway specifically
  requires histology.

### LR-M (Malignant, not HCC-specific)
- **Action**: Biopsy required for definitive diagnosis. Multidisciplinary
  discussion.

### LR-TIV (Tumour in vein)
- **Action**: Multidisciplinary discussion. Indicates advanced disease;
  imaging diagnosis sufficient if associated with parenchymal HCC. Biopsy
  if origin uncertain.

### LR-NC (Not categorisable)
- **Action**: Repeat imaging with optimised technique. State the specific
  technical limitation that prevented categorisation.

## Worked Examples

**Example 1**: Cirrhotic patient. 14 mm hepatic observation in segment 8
with non-rim APHE and non-peripheral washout. No capsule, no prior
imaging.
→ Non-rim APHE + washout + ≥ 10 mm = **LR-5**.
→ **HCC management pathway. Biopsy not required.**

**Example 2**: Cirrhotic patient. 18 mm hepatic observation with non-rim
APHE only, no washout, no capsule, no prior imaging for comparison.
→ ≥ 10 mm with APHE alone, < 20 mm = **LR-4**.
→ **Multidisciplinary discussion. Options include repeat imaging,
biopsy, or treatment without biopsy.**

**Example 3**: Cirrhotic patient. 8 mm observation with non-rim APHE,
no other features.
→ < 10 mm with APHE alone = **LR-3**.
→ **Repeat or alternative diagnostic imaging in 3-6 months.**

**Example 4**: Cirrhotic patient. 35 mm targetoid lesion with rim APHE,
peripheral washout, marked diffusion restriction.
→ Targetoid pattern = **LR-M**.
→ **Biopsy required. Multidisciplinary discussion.**

**Example 5**: Patient with no risk factors for HCC. 12 mm hepatic
lesion with arterial enhancement and washout.
→ LI-RADS does NOT apply. Characterise with standard liver imaging
principles (FNH, adenoma, metastasis, etc.) — do NOT assign an LR
category.

## Output Requirements for Impressions

When applying LI-RADS:
1. Confirm the patient meets LI-RADS at-risk criteria. If at-risk status
   is not stated in the findings, note that explicitly: "LI-RADS
   categorisation assumes at-risk status, which was not stated."
2. State the LR category and the major features that support it.
3. State the specific management action (HCC pathway, MDD, repeat
   imaging in 3-6 months, return to surveillance) — do NOT use vague
   phrases like "consider biopsy" or "further evaluation."
4. **Never recommend biopsy for LR-5** unless the findings explicitly
   raise diagnostic uncertainty.
5. State the surveillance interval explicitly when applicable
   (e.g. "every 6 months").
